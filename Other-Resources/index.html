Useful sites and resources to use during the college process

Other Resources

All of the content I provide is not sufficient as your only resource throughout your college process. My site covers the intangibles well, but does not suffice as a resource for deciding on specific colleges, planning your major, etc.

I will recommend some resources that can be useful depending on what you are looking for.

College Board

Being the powerhouse of the college application industry, College Board does have some useful resources on their website. They range from SAT prep to college planning and searching.

At the very least you can expect the content on College Board to be reliable.

Forum Sites

Forum sites (like the subreddits on Reddit or content on College Confidential) can be useful when used with a healthy amount of skepticism. They are forum sites so you have to be careful to not take every post as fact or true. Still, though, it gives access to a lot of students, parents, and guidance professionals; some posts can be helpful and informative.

It is nice to hear actual students' and professionals' experiences and opinions of different colleges. You will come across individual biases, but this is nullified with enough opinions and posts. It can function as a good supplementary resource or as a means to ask specific questions, and along with guidance professionals and other resources it can help you during the college process.

This isn't limited to college forum sites, but be careful of comparing yourself to other applicants. It is impossible to have all the information and it only produces more anxiety for you during the process.

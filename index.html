Why Is This Site Different?

In 2017, 80+% of students who apply to top schools are academically qualified. Colleges choose between students with similar GPA’s, scores, and often times extracurriculars. How do they choose?

Beyond grades and scores lie the intangibles that admissions officers look for. Traits like intellectual curiosity and kindness are often the differentiating factors between the student with a 4.0 and 1480 SAT getting accepted over another with a 4.0 and 1480.

This website shows you how to showcase and leverage the traits that you possess that are the most desirable to admissions officers.

This site, unlike most college admissions sites, focuses on the factors beyond grades, scores, and extra-curriculars that translate to acceptances and bigger merit aid packages.

I got into an Ivy League School, Dartmouth College, from that 80% of academically qualified. Nothing I did was extraordinary and I am neither a recruited athlete nor ‘hooked’ applicant (with a compelling major factor in admission ie: minority, first generation, legacy, low income, etc) nor was I even in the top 1% of my public high school.

Dartmouth accepts 10% of applicants. They differentiate through these intangibles.

I see students work hard all through high school only to neglect to show these desired qualities to colleges. They fail to understand these nuances and, so, become one of the many 'academically qualified' rejects.

I hope to provide access to all of the important information on the inexact factors in college admissions.

What Can You Even Offer?

As I went through the college application process in 2016 I became obsessed with learning the nuances of the process. I wanted to know how admissions officers at top schools choose between applicants whose grades and scores are all stellar. Some of these intangibles I was aware of throughout the process but some I began to understand more thoroughly in hind-sight.

Analyzing the admission results of those around me, it saddened me when I saw friends and peers just as academically competent as me or others accepted to top schools fail to get in because they didn't understand all these nuances and hidden factors.

It is my goal to educate you on these aspects. The admission process continues to fascinate me and I hope to leverage this fascination to help you through your application process.

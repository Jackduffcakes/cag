Standing Out
In The College Process
"the great majority of students who are admitted stand out from the rest because a lot of little things, when added up, tip the scale in their favor"
  -Yale Admissions Website
How Is This Site Different?

In 2017, the majority of applicants to any particular college have similar grades, scores, and often extracurriculars (explanation
Some applicants fall below (it's their 'reach' school) and some above (it's their 'safety' school), but the majority usually have similar grades and scores. For example, admissions officers from top schools will often say 'most applicants are academically qualified.' This is supported by top schools' application statistics which show that most applicants- who have scores and grades in the school's average range (middle 50%)- won't be accepted. The similarity of grades and scores for the majority of applicants forces most colleges (not just top schools) to look to other factors to make admissions decisions.
)
. How do admissions officers choose?

Beyond grades and scores lie the "little things," the intangibles, that admissions officers look for. Traits like intellectual curiosity and intrinsic motivation are often the differentiating factors between applicants.

For colleges across America, there are always students with similar stats that admissions officers are forced to choose between.



This site focuses on the factors beyond grades, scores, and extra-curriculars that translate to acceptances and bigger merit aid packages.


Students work hard all through high school only to neglect to show these desired qualities to colleges and, so, become one of many 'academically qualified' rejects.

Standing Out
In The College Process

"the great majority of students who are admitted stand out from the rest because a lot of little things, when added up, tip the scale in their favor"
  -Yale Admissions Website
How Is This Site Different?

In 2017, 80+% of students who apply to top schools are academically qualified. Colleges choose between students with similar GPA’s, scores, and often extracurriculars. How do they choose?


Beyond grades and scores lie the "little things," the intangibles, that admissions officers look for. Traits like intellectual curiosity and intrinsic motivation are often the differentiating factors between the student with a 4.0 and 1480 SAT getting accepted over another with a 4.0 and 1480.


This site focuses on the factors beyond grades, scores, and extra-curriculars that translate to acceptances and bigger merit aid packages.


Students work hard all through high school only to neglect to show these desired qualities to colleges. They fail to understand these nuances and become one of many 'academically qualified' rejects.


It is my goal to educate you on the factors that differentiate college applicants.
